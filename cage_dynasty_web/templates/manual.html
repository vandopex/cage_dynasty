⚔️ Winning Fights

Fights are decided by the attributes of both fighters plus the circumstances of the matchup. A boxer with elite boxing skill will usually outperform one with average skill — but style matchups matter too.
Strikers are dangerous on the feet. Wrestlers can take the fight to the ground and neutralize striking. BJJ specialists hunt for submissions once the fight is on the mat.
Key stats that drive fight outcomes
Striking — Boxing, Kicks, Clinch Striking decide how much damage you deal standing up.
Defense — Striking Defense, Takedown Defense determine how much you absorb.
Grappling — Takedowns gets you to the ground, Top Control keeps you there, Submissions finishes from dominant position.
Physical — Cardio matters in late rounds; tired fighters make mistakes and get finished. Chin determines how well you absorb big shots. Strength affects grappling leverage.
Mental — Fight IQ and Composure affect decision-making under pressure. Heart determines whether a fighter survives adversity or quits.
Finishes happen when a fighter takes too much damage (KO/TKO), gets caught in a submission they can't escape (SUB), or can't continue due to accumulated punishment. Decisions go to judges who score each round independently.
{# ── SECTION 2: Building a Fighter ────────────────────────── #}

🥊 Building a Fighter

Every fighter has a potential ceiling — the maximum level they can reach with perfect training. Some fighters are high-ceiling prospects who need years to develop. Others are ready now but won't get much better.
Overall Rating (OVR) is a summary number for display only. It does not directly affect fights — the individual attribute values do.
Facilities limit how high your fighters can develop. A GARAGE tier camp can only develop fighters to a certain point. Upgrading your facility raises that ceiling.
{# ── SECTION 3: Training & Development ────────────────────── #}

💪 Training & Development

Each week you set a focus (what to train) and an intensity (how hard). Higher intensity produces bigger gains but increases fatigue. Training the same stat repeatedly is more efficient than switching constantly.
Decay: stats you aren't training will slowly decay over time. Set decay floors to protect minimums — once a stat hits its floor, the system auto-trains it to prevent further decay.
The Training Queue lets you set multi-week goals and walk away. Set Boxing → 82, then Kicks → 78, then Maintain. The system executes automatically week by week.
Style Templates load a full training plan designed for your fighter's fighting style with one click.
Auto-rest: if your fighter's fatigue gets too high, the system will automatically reduce intensity or force rest to protect their condition. You'll see this on the news feed.
{# ── SECTION 4: Running Your Camp ─────────────────────────── #}

🏗️ Running Your Camp

Your camp balance is your income minus expenses. Purses come from fights — the better the slot on the card, the more you earn. Main events pay more than prelims.
Coaches improve your training gains. A higher-rated coach with a specialty matching your training focus produces better results.
Fight of the Night bonuses ($50,000 each) are awarded to the fighters in the most exciting fight on the card. These are meaningful income early on.
Watch your runway — weeks of expenses covered by your current balance. If you run out of money, you can't operate your camp.
{# ── SECTION 5: Rankings & Titles ─────────────────────────── #}

🏆 Rankings & Titles

Beat ranked opponents to enter and climb the rankings. Win streaks help. Losing drops you. Being ranked #1 or #2 makes you a title contender — a title fight offer will come.
Champions defend their title against the top contender. Losing a title fight costs you the belt and drops your ranking. The champion's record matters — long reign champions become legends.
{# ── SECTION 6: Amateur Circuit ───────────────────────────── #}

🥋 Amateur Circuit

The amateur circuit runs independently. Fighters develop, compete, and some emerge as prospects worth signing.
Browse the Amateur Circuit page to find prospects. Better prospects have higher potential ceilings and stronger amateur records. Signing them costs nothing but they need time to develop before they're ready to compete professionally.
{# ── SECTION 7: Reading the Interface ─────────────────────── #}

👁️ Reading the Interface

Rankings: the number next to a fighter's name (#3, #7) is their division ranking. Unranked fighters haven't beaten anyone ranked yet.
Method abbreviations
KO — knockout
TKO — technical knockout (stopped by ref / corner / doctor)
SUB — submission
DEC — decision (went to judges)
SPLIT DEC — one judge scored it differently
News feed: stories about fights, injuries, retirements, and notable performances across all divisions. The game world is always active — other organizations are running events, fighters are developing, champions are defending.
FOTN = Fight of the Night. Automatically awarded to the most exciting fight on each card.
OVR = Overall Rating. A display number that summarizes a fighter's attribute total. Useful for quick comparisons but not used by the fight engine directly.
